Price Asymmetry In the Oil Industry

This article dives into why gas prices fluctuate so often and how it affects you.

What is oil price asymmetry?

Oil price asymmetry is a commonly practiced tactic in the oil industry to maximize profits while lowering risk of investment into crude oil. Essentially the companies are much more eager to raise the price of gasoline in response to an increase of crude oil price, whereas if there is a price decrease in crude oil they are less likely to respond by decreasing the price. Most all scholars that investigate this issue say it is the main cause of flux in the gasoline market (see references).

How does it affect me?

It doesn't just affect one person, it affects everyone. Gasoline is an inelastic commodity which means that people will always buy it no matter the price because you need it. People need gasoline to get to work, to travel across the country, or to carpool to school; everyone uses it and everyone is affected by the price of it. The price goes up and you have no other choice other than to pay for it if you want to travel.

How can I help?

The best way that anyone can help is by emailing, calling, or going to your local legislator about this problem and informing them of it. If enough people talk about it then there can be change! Tell your legislator that gas prices are unfair to the people and that they should make the oil industry price more fairly!
